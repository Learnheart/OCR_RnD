## Sticking to it

Vancouver PWHL showcase hits up fond feelings for Victoria star Poulin

M AY 2024 — The Vancouver PWHL team’s first home game of the season was a celebration of the past, present and future of women’s hockey in British Columbia. The game, held at the Langley Events Centre, featured a special tribute to Victoria’s own star player, Marie-Philip Poulin, who was honored for her contributions to the sport and her role in inspiring the next generation of female athletes.

Poulin, who played for the Vancouver Canucks in the NHL and later joined the PWHL, was a guest speaker at the pre-game ceremony. She spoke about her journey from a young girl playing street hockey in Victoria to becoming one of Canada’s most decorated players. “It’s incredible to see how far we’ve come,” she said. “The game has evolved, and so have the opportunities for girls and women to play at the highest levels.”

The game itself was a showcase of talent and teamwork. The Vancouver PWHL team, featuring players from across British Columbia, demonstrated their skills with powerful shots, precise passes, and dynamic plays. The crowd, which included many local fans and young girls who had come to watch the game, cheered loudly throughout the match.

In the second half, the team’s star forward, Marie-Philip Poulin, scored a goal that sent the crowd into a frenzy. Her shot was a perfect blend of power and precision, and it was met with a thunderous ovation from the fans. “That goal was pure magic,” said one fan who had come to see Poulin play. “She’s not just a great player — she’s a role model.”

The game ended in a 3-2 victory for Vancouver, but the real win was the celebration of women’s hockey and the inspiring story of Marie-Philip Poulin. The team’s coach, Scott, praised the players for their hard work and dedication. “We’re not just playing for wins,” he said. “We’re playing for the future of women’s hockey in this province.”

The game was also a reminder of the importance of balancing multiple commitments. Scott, who is both a player and a coach, has to juggle his time between training, games, and his responsibilities at UVic. “It’s not easy,” he admitted. “But I love what I do, and I’m committed to making the most of every opportunity.”

The Vancouver PWHL team’s first home game was a success on many levels — a celebration of the past, present and future of women’s hockey in British Columbia, and a testament to the power of teamwork and dedication. As the team prepares for their next game, they are already looking ahead to the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead. “We’re not just playing for wins,” Scott said. “We’re playing for the future of women’s hockey in this province.”
