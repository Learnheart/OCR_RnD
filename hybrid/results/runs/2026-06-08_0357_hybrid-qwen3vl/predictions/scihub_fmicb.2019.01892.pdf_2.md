## Isolation of Bacteria and Fungi

Soil samples were homogenized and sterilized in sterile 10 ml saline, prior to inoculation. Root and soil samples were collected and sterilized after which the roots were removed from the MgSO₄, the resulting soil pellet after centrifugation (15 min, 3700g) constituted the rhizosphere fraction. Poplar roots were surface sterilized by immersing them for 5 min in 5% NaClO and bacteriostatic rinses were sterilized with 0.1% NaClO supplemented with 0.1% Tween 20. The roots were rinsed with sterile water and then dried with sterile filter paper. The roots were then placed in sterile tubes and stored at 4°C until use. The surface sterilized roots were amended in 15 ml sterile 10 mM MgSO₄, and stored for 1 min using a Polypropylene (PP) tube (Kimaxartica).

For fungal isolation, dilutions up to 10⁻⁵ of the soil and rhizosphere and endophytic root samples were plated onto potato dextrose agar (PDA) medium pH 5.5, as described by Dierickx and Lamm (1996), and incubated for 7 days at 25°C. After incubation, fungal mycelia were selected, purified and stored on solid malt extract (2% w/v) medium (30°C, 7 days).

For bacterial isolation, dilutions of the soil samples were made at a selective minimal medium (Gibson et al., 1998) supplemented with 25 mg l⁻¹ chloramphenicol as sole carbon source, or with 5 mM glucose in addition to chloramphenicol. Every 10 days, 10 ml of the culture was transferred into 90 ml of fresh medium with a slightly higher concentration of chloramphenicol, ultimately leading to 10 mg l⁻¹ chloramphenicol after 3 months of incubation at 30°C and 120 rpm. At the end of the experiment, chloramphenicol concentration decreased to 10 mg l⁻¹ using High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC). The number of bacterial colonies were purified, stored and identified using 15% (w/v) glycerol, 0.6% NaCl (w/v) at -40°C.

## Identification of Isolated Bacteria and Fungi

DNA was extracted from bacterial isolates using the DNeasy Blood and Tissue Kit (Qiagen, Venlo, Netherlands) and fungal isolates using the DNeasy® PowerSoil™ Kit (Qiagen, Venlo, Netherlands).
