# OUEPINEING 97

Gota lori, kava kava, St. John’s wort, valerian may increase CNS depression (see Table). Some LAB tests may be altered. May increase total free thyroid hormone levels. May increase serum cholesterol, triglycerides, ALT, AST, WBC, GGT, IOT. May cause false positive pregnancy test.

## ADMINISTRATION/HANDLING

- Give immediate-release tablets without regard to food. *Do not break, crush, dissolve, or divide extended-release tablets.* Extended-release tablets should be given without regard to food or with a light meal in evening.

## INDICATIONS/ROUTINES/DOSAGE

Note: When restarting patients who have been on QEUINING for less than 12 months, it is not required and maintenance dose is recommended. *When restarting patients who have been on QEUINING for longer than 12 months, the starting dose schedule.* When discontinuing, gradual tapering recommended to avoid withdrawal symptoms and minimize risk of relapse.

### Gota lori

**IMMEDIATE-RELEASE:** ADULTS: Elderly: Initially 25 mg twice daily; increase to 50–100 mg twice daily (divided 2–3 times/day) on the second day, up to 300–400 mg twice daily on the third day, then to 50–100 mg twice daily on the fourth day. Further adjustments to 25–50 mg twice daily may be made at intervals of 2 days or less. Maximum dose: 150–250 mg twice daily (adults); 50–200 mg (elderly). For patients with renal impairment, initial dose is 25 mg twice daily. May increase as intervals as short as 1 day to 300–400 mg. Range: 600–800 mg/day. Maximum: 1,200 mg/day.

**EXTENDED-RELEASE:** ADULTS: Elderly: Initially 50 mg twice daily; increase to 100 mg twice daily on the second day, then to 200 mg twice daily on the third day, then to 300 mg twice daily on the fourth day. Maximum: 600 mg/day. Elderly: Initially 25 mg twice daily; increase to 50 mg twice daily on the second day, then to 100 mg twice daily on the third day, then to 200 mg twice daily on the fourth day. Maximum: 400 mg/day.
