\hat{W} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \hat{B} + \hat{B}^* \right) = \frac{\text{tr}(\hat{B})}{2} I.

where $I: \Lambda^k \to \Lambda^k$ is the identity operator. Using (10) and the fact that $*$ is $(-1)$-self-adjoint, it follows easily that the eigenspaces $\Lambda^k$ are $W$-invariant.

Along with $\Lambda^k$, there is a decomposition $W = W^+ \oplus W^-$ into endomorphisms $W^+, W^-: \Lambda^k \to \Lambda^k$, respectively. An oriented Riemannian 4-manifold is said to be self-dual if $W^+ = 0$ and anti-self-dual if $W^- = 0$. In terms of (9), $W^+ = 0$ means $A = \pm B$ (see [14–15] below), recalling that $W = \text{curl}^2 B$. It is clear that these are conformally invariant conditions.

The final step that we will need is to define the space of self-dual 2-forms. To do this, let $G_{+k}$ denote the set of decomposable 2-vectors of $(\Lambda^k \otimes \Lambda^k)$, i.e., all 2-vectors $\xi \in \Lambda^2$ of the form $\xi = c \wedge v$ for some $v \in T_xM$, with $\xi \in G_{+k}$, is also called 2-plane of $T_xM$. These can be characterized by a dimension four: $G_{+k} = \{ \xi^* \in \Lambda^2, (\xi^*, \xi^*) = 0, (\xi^*, \xi^*) = 1/2 \}$, see e.g., [3706]. The quadratic form of $W$ is then the map defined by
$$
T \mapsto (W, P_{+k})_k, \quad P \in G_{+k}.
$$
(Note: its relation to (9), exact 2-forms, and to the Levi-Civita connection in [7606] is a matter of interest.) In particular, the condition $W(T, \cdot) = 0$ will play a fundamental role in our study of the condition $W(T, \cdot) = 0$, to which we now turn.

3. THE CONDITION $W(T, \cdot) = 0$

We now proceed to our objects of study: certain Riemannian 4-manifolds $(M, g)$ whose Weyl tensors $W$ satisfy the pointwise condition
$$
W(T, \cdot) = 0, \quad \text{some unit } T \in T_xM.
$$
This is a conformally invariant condition, trivial if $g$ is locally conformally flat. For example, consider the case where $M = (\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ with $g = (dx^1)^2 + (dx^2)^2 + (dx^3)^2 + (dx^4)^2$ by
$$
g = (2x^1 dx^2 + dx^3 dx^4) \otimes (2x^1 dx^2 + dx^3 dx^4) + \cdots.
$$
The components of the Weyl tensor of $g$ relative to the orthonormal basis $(e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4) = (\partial/\partial x^1, \partial/\partial x^2, \partial/\partial x^3, \partial/\partial x^4)$ are given by
$$
\begin{array}{c|cccc}
 & e_1 & e_2 & e_3 & e_4 \\
\hline
e_1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
e_2 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
e_3 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
e_4 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\end{array}
$$
It can be verified that $T = \partial/\partial x^1$ satisfies $W(T, \cdot) = 0$ on $M$. On the other hand, there are no such Riemannian 4-manifolds that are not locally conformally flat but nevertheless satisfy (12).
